The problem

Manually building rosters is slow, and spreadsheets make the tradeoffs invisible.

  • schedule
    Everything fights everything

    Teammate requests, availability, role caps, skill balance, and “keep these people apart” all pull in different directions.

  • table
    Policies hide in cells

    The sheet holds names and numbers. The real rules live in your head — or in a note nobody updates.

  • replay
    One change breaks three others

    Move one person and you redo half the grid. Fairness becomes guesswork, and you never know what you traded off.

Why RapidRoster

RapidRoster lets organizers state those tradeoffs as an explicit rule list — then finds placements that respect hard constraints and score best on the soft ones.

Import people and slots, set priorities, generate assignments, review what was traded off, and export when it feels right. Projects save so you can leave and come back.

See it in the product

Setup, rules, generate, and results — one workspace for every kind of roster.

Four building-block rules

Compose groupings from a few fundamental rules — not dozens of one-off settings.

  1. group_work
    Cluster

    Keep matched people together, or place someone in a matching slot — teammate requests, preference columns, overlapping availability.

  2. call_split
    Separate

    Spread similar participants apart — schools across teams, experienced players, people who should not share a slot.

  3. filter_alt
    Limit

    Cap or require counts per slot — maximum 2 keepers, minimum 1 coach.

  4. balance
    Balance

    Even out a number across slots — skill, age, or any other score you track.

Soft rules guide the search by score (partial success still counts). Hard rules never allow illegal placements. Mark priority from 1–10.

How it works

  1. Sign in and create a project

    Save people, slots, rules, and results — come back later.

  2. Import people and slots

    CSV in, column types set, sizes and conflict groups configured.

  3. Add rules

    Cluster, Separate, Limit, Balance — with priority and hard/soft.

  4. Generate, review, export

    Primary run plus alternatives, satisfaction report, download CSV.
